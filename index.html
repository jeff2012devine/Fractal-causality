Synchronization Threshold in Coupled Logistic Maps
Reproducible Lyapunov analysis (LLE & TLE) shows a sustained transverse crossing at ε ≈ 0.3462 for mean-field coupling while LLE remains positive — synchronized chaos.
View on Zenodo (DOI) Download White Paper (PDF) View Figure

Summary

We reproduce classic results on synchronization transitions in coupled map lattices (CMLs), following Kaneko (Physica D, 1990). Using Benettin’s method, we compute the largest Lyapunov exponent (LLE) and the transverse Lyapunov exponent (TLE) in coupled logistic maps. In mean-field coupling, TLE crosses below zero at ε ≈ 0.3462 while LLE stays ≈0.45–0.50.

How to Reproduce (Colab)

  1. Open Google Colab and create a new notebook.
  2. Paste the one-cell “mean-field LLE+TLE sweep” block from the paper/notebook.
  3. Run the cell. You should see the LLE/TLE curves and a crossing near ε ≈ 0.346.

For tighter estimates near the crossing, increase measure_T, try a larger L, and average over 2–3 seeds.
